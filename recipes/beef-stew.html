Beef-stew recipe

Description

Beef is slowly stewed in tomatoes, curry powder, and other spices to give you some hearty and fork-tender stew to serve alongside some East African pilau or your favorite side dishes!

You can make this beef stew on the stovetop or using your Instant Pot or Pressure Cooker.

ingredients

  • beef
  • carrots
  • onions
  • curry powder
  • black pepper
  • smoked paprika
  • tumeric

steps

  1. Cut your beef into stewing chunks. Season it with a teaspoon of salt, curry powder, turmeric, smoked paprika, and black pepper, and allow it to marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight for best results.
  2. Using your blender or hand mixer, process onion, the remaining salt, tomato, red bell pepper, and tomato puree. Use a pestle and mortar to grind the garlic and ginger. Alternatively, process the garlic and ginger with the tomatoes in the blender if you prefer.
  3. Under high heat, heat a medium-sized pot or pan then add the oil. Add the beef chunks and allow them to brown (about 2-3 minutes) on each side. Turn and allow the other side to brown for about 2 minutes, then stir in the garlic and ginger. Fry for a further 2 minutes until fragrant then add the blended tomato mixture and stir.

    If making this on the stovetop, simmer for 40-45 minutes, stirring from time to time. You can add more water if you prefer your stew to have more sauce.

  4. Serve this Kenyan beef stew with some east african pilau or your favorite side dish.
